- Deciding which of the many equivalent formulations of a causal system to adopt is not that different from choosing between equivalent formulations of a hard science system. We share the opinion that there is an underlying, simpler truth.
    
- In trying to understand the softer sciences you might be impressed by how hard it is to set out an exemplar of a shining indisputable truth. But don't let that make you believe that in social science, anything goes. Dont forget how _easy_ it is to set out an exemplar of a shining indisputable _untruth_ like "no women can perceive the colour blue" or "all Democrats are liars".
