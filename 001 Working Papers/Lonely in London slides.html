The experiment

Human wrapper + AI inner paper

Topic: Loneliness among young adults in London

  • I have no “skin in the game”

The Cursor workspace (a coding app, not QDA software)

Cursor provides a single agent within the workspace, which can access the source files and create and update memo files. In this case its model was GPT-5.1.

Substantive findings (AI paper)

Themes and mechanisms

Theme Mechanism Example
Misrecognition Near others yet unseen “I’ve always had people around me physically, but I haven’t felt like they can understand”
Place Crowded anonymity, threat “Better lonely than scared”
Infrastructure Low-judgement repeated contact “I sometimes prefer opening up to strangers because they don’t know me as much”
Filters Money, apps “They know that I don’t have money”

Implication: Build low-cost, low-judgement spaces; address safety and mobility as gating conditions.

Connection infrastructure

Thick - Teams, centres, faith communities - Ongoing ties, advice networks

Thin - Everyday third spaces - Known strangers (gym, hairdresser)

Relief ≠ deep intimacy. Minimal unit: recognisability, low-stakes talk.

Evaluation

Reflection

It worked 🎇🎇! It really developed a theory iteratively!

I enjoyed watching Cursor work, step by step creating and updating its memos.

I find the paper unreadable.

Lack of skin in the game: I don’t care.

Easy to think ‘it looks systematic, so it must be right’.

WEIRD

Like all such methods, extremely exposed to the WEIRDness of the training data.

(Alternative approach: causal mapping)

Top 30 links. Completely automated/generic approach. 30 minutes to auto-code. 5 minutes to filter. Much less exposure to LLM world-view.

Bookmark #1319

Finally

  • Yes an AI can iterate a theory using memos
  • You don’t need a human to generate a theory
  • The process was lacking “skin in the game”
  • Could we give the AI some skin in the game? Not at the moment.
  • Could we give the AI the opportunity to reflect? Absolutely.
  • This automated approach is a bit more reproducible and auditable
  • Still very exposed to WEIRDness

Takeaways

  1. Cursor’s affordance: Self-updating memo files enable iterative thematic analysis beyond single-prompt approaches
  2. Substantive contribution: Loneliness as infrastructure + constraint, not individual deficit
  3. Transparency: Quote-checking and methodology audit revealed overclaiming — valuable for AI-in-research reporting
  4. Design target: “Safer and more predictable interaction with lower judgement costs”
