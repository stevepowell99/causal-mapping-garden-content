A Multi-Method Power Tool

Causal Mapping isn't primarily an evaluation method; it's a way of identifying and organising causal claims in the service of evaluative judgement. It fits really well with Contribution Analysis (CA), but its systematic approach adds value to any evidence-based framework:

✔
Outcome Harvesting: Strengthening "significance" descriptions by mapping the precise causal chains from outcome back to intervention.
✔
Realist Evaluation: Helping to identify the specific Context-Mechanism-Outcome (CMO) linkages mentioned by sources.
✔
Theory of Change: Testing pre-defined theories against hundreds of empirical "mini-theories" from stakeholders.

1 Link = 100 Claims

In a causal map, an arrow from "Training" to "Knowledge" might look simple. But behind that single arrow sits a database of perhaps dozens or hundreds of specific, auditable claims from different sources.

Verifiability at Scale

Causal Mapping allows you to "double-click" on any part of your summary map to see the weight of evidence. It moves evaluation away from cherry-picked quotes toward systematic evidence density.

  • Each bar represents the number of unique claims supporting a link.
  • Includes diverse sources (Farmers, Staff, Partners).
  • Every claim is linked back to the verbatim quote.

Integrating causal mapping into Mayne’s 6 Steps

1

Set out the Attribution Problem

Define the evaluation questions and the level of evidence required.

CM Win: Identifies all potential causal pathways mentioned in the data.
2

Develop the Theory of Change

Establish the logic of how the intervention is expected to lead to results.

CM Win: Reconstructs the empirical ToC from hundreds of stakeholder views.
3

Gather Evidence on the ToC

Collect existing and new evidence to populate the causal links.

CM Win: Catalogues & labels causal claims from texts systematically.
4

Assemble the Performance Story

Build the narrative of contribution based on the synthesized evidence.

CM Win: Synthesizes individual claims into long, verifiable chains.
5

Assess Alternative Explanations

Account for external influences and other drivers of the observed change.

CM Win: Explicitly maps non-project influences mentioned by sources.
6

Revise & Strengthen

Refine the story based on gaps identified in the evidence base.

CM Win: Highlights "weak links" where evidence count is low.

Scalable Qualitative Analysis

When you move from 10 interviews to 200 reports, "manual coding" fails. Causal mapping uses AI not to replace the evaluator, but to automate the extraction of verifiable evidence nuggets.
